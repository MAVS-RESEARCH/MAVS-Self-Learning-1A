# Baseline Source and Fidelity Registry

## Phase 1 policy

All Phase 1 implementations are labeled benchmark adaptations, not author-code reproductions. They share the inherited `GovernanceMethod` contract, receive identical visible opportunities and released feedback, declare their grids and resource budgets in `configs/baselines/phase1_dynamic.yaml`, and are selected only on the development bank. The final evaluation does not claim the original papers' formal guarantees because the benchmark decision semantics, synthetic scores, finite windows, and delayed/censored feedback differ from those papers' assumptions.

| Phase 1 mechanism | Governing primary source | Preserved mechanism | Adaptation/deviation | Information and fidelity test |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Confidence, entropy, margin, generalized selective classification | [Selective Classification Under Distribution Shifts](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.05160) | Confidence/margin-based abstention under label and covariate shift | Three-action accept/reject/escalate mapping replaces binary predict/abstain evaluation | Visible score, entropy, margin, evidence quality, disagreement; tests enforce no hidden labels and fixed thresholds |
| Neyman-Pearson and cost-sensitive selection | [Neyman-Pearson likelihood-ratio principle](https://doi.org/10.1098/rsta.1933.0009) | Likelihood-ratio ordering and explicit asymmetric cost | Synthetic risk odds substitute for known class densities; reported as an adaptation | Visible risk and declared cost preference only; sweep and action-boundary tests |
| Split conformal and conformal risk control | [Conformal Risk Control](https://arxiv.org/abs/2208.02814) | Separate calibration scores, monotone risk threshold, frozen split/CRC variants | Binary governance risk and escalation band replace prediction sets; no distribution-free claim is transferred | Disjoint calibration bank; calibration count/hash and threshold invariance tests |
| Adaptive and online conformal | [Adaptive Conformal Inference Under Distribution Shift](https://papers.neurips.cc/paper_files/paper/2021/hash/0d441de75945e5acbc865406fc9a2559-Abstract.html), [Online Conformal Prediction with Decaying Step Sizes](https://proceedings.mlr.press/v235/angelopoulos24a.html) | Released-score online adjustment under non-stationarity | Bounded residual-quantile update is a benchmark proxy, not the papers' full prediction-set construction | Released feedback only; censored/delayed causality and threshold-update tests |
| ADWIN | [Learning from Time-Changing Data with Adaptive Windowing](https://doi.org/10.1137/1.9781611972771.42) | Variable window, two-subwindow mean test, Hoeffding-style bound, stale-prefix removal | Monitors governance residual rather than classifier error; bounded 128-event memory | Released residuals only; drift/reset and memory tests |
| Page-Hinkley | [Inference about the change-point from cumulative sum tests](https://academic.oup.com/biomet/article-abstract/58/3/509/233432) | Cumulative mean-deviation change statistic and reset | Uses bounded governance residual and fixed development-selected alarm level | Released residuals only; no-future-feedback and alarm tests |
| Hedge/Exponentiated Weights | [A Decision-Theoretic Generalization of On-Line Learning and an Application to Boosting](https://doi.org/10.1006/jcss.1997.1504) | Multiplicative loss-weight updates over fixed approved experts | Deterministic argmax routing is used instead of randomized expert sampling | Released outcomes and three fixed thresholds; weight normalization/regret tests |
| Continual test-time adaptation | [Continual Test-Time Domain Adaptation](https://openaccess.thecvf.com/content/CVPR2022/html/Wang_Continual_Test-Time_Domain_Adaptation_CVPR_2022_paper.html) | Frozen source predictor plus entropy or confidence-filtered pseudo-label adaptation, reset/recovery evaluation | Only a bounded output-bias proxy adapts; no augmentation averaging or stochastic neuron restoration; therefore labeled a CTTA proxy | Current visible features and released feedback only; source checkpoint frozen on disjoint domains and tested on changed-prior evaluation |
| Context-selected fixed configurations | Architecture authority, Section 13 | Select only among approved configurations and fall back through governed thresholds | Deterministic domain/cost table; no learned selector | Visible domain and cost preference; fixed-state and scope tests |

The Phase 1 source predictor and every adaptive method remain fixed-representation comparators. Their behavior cannot support a claim that the feasible governance frontier expanded.
