# Claim Boundary

## Phase 0 status

Phase 0 is infrastructure and measurement-integrity validation only. Its outputs
may establish deterministic generation, trace/schema/hash integrity, matched
opportunity delivery, hidden-state separation, metric identities, and diagnostic
bounds. They do not establish that MAVS-Self-Learning is safer, more accurate,
more efficient, more robust, Pareto-superior, or capable of compounding.

Accept-all, reject-all, escalate-all, random, oracle-label, and oracle-regime are
non-competitive diagnostic bounds. Oracle outputs use evaluator-only information
and are categorically excluded from method comparisons.

No model is trained in Phase 0. Consequently, Phase 0 has no training benchmark,
model checkpoint, or model-performance claim. Later trained components remain
subject to the disjoint training/certification/final policy in `WorkPlan.md`.

## Phase 4 automated claim policy

Phase 4 publishes the complete frozen sweep and may report descriptive operating-point, frontier, paired-world, calibration, regret, burden, catastrophic-event, adaptation, recovery, recurrence, and baseline-win measurements. Diagnostic and oracle points remain excluded from competitive claims.

A MAVS-SL superiority statement is permitted only when the generated `phase4_summary.json` records every fail-closed gate as true: positive paired hypervolume improvement, lower UAR at matched FRR, lower FRR at matched UAR, no hidden escalation/intervention/latency/compute regression, more than one favorable operating point, reject-all exclusion, complete sweep publication, complete trace lineage, and recorded Holm family-wise correction. If any gate fails, the only permitted verdict is `NOT_SUPPORTED`; a single favorable threshold, seed, scalarization, or reject-all solution is never sufficient.

No model is trained or tuned in Phase 4. All operating points are frozen before final-bank compilation, and final results cannot be used to modify the registry.
