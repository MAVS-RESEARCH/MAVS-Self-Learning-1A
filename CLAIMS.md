## Benchmark-scoped generalization

The results support **generalization within the preregistered Version 0.4 benchmark family**: the cumulative system transfers from the retrospective development lineage to a separately seeded, sealed blind bank containing disjoint world identities and surface, structural, and adversarial realizations. On that blind bank, G2/G3 preserve observed UAR and FRR at zero, preserve zero scope leakage, and reduce closure burden relative to fresh learning.

This is evidence of held-out benchmark generalization across the declared Phase 9 families, generation roles, reset mixtures, and resource budgets. It is not evidence of unrestricted generalization to arbitrary domains, distributions, tools, policies, or production environments.

Unsupported:

- universal zero error or zero escalation outside the certified covered class;
- production deployment readiness;
- general-domain, out-of-benchmark, or frontier-model superiority.

Oracle conditions are evaluator-only bounds and are never competitive claims. Earlier Phase 3 evidence validates lifecycle plumbing, not genuine differentiated synthesis. Rejected Phase 10 runs are diagnostic-only and cannot elevate a claim.
